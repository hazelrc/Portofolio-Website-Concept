Favourite Thing

My laptop

Honestly, at first I didn’t really know what my favorite thing was. I kept thinking about it and nothing came to mind right away. But then I realized that my favorite thing is actually something I use every single day without even noticing how important it is, which is my laptop. It sounds simple, but when I think about it, my laptop is the one thing that I can’t really live without.

My laptop is basically the center of everything I do. First of all, it’s for studying. I use it to open lecture slides, read notes, and search for extra materials online. Whenever I have assignments, I do everything on my laptop, whether it’s writing essays, making presentations, or working on group projects with classmates. I also spend a lot of time on YouTube. Sometimes I watch tutorials to learn new things, and other times I just watch random videos to relax and pass the time.

Of course, it’s not all about school. My laptop is also what I use to have fun. I play games when I’m bored, watch movies when I want to chill, and listen to music whenever I need a break. On top of that, I sometimes use it for personal projects, like editing videos, trying out coding, or just experimenting with creative ideas. It feels like one device that can handle everything I need in a day, from being productive to simply relaxing.

Favourite Activity

Travelling and Making Cinematic Videos

I really enjoy traveling because it makes me feel calm and free. Daily life can get pretty repetitive, so going to new places feels like hitting the reset button for my mind. I love exploring new areas, trying different activities, and just experiencing a fresh vibe. For me, traveling isn’t only about moving from one location to another, it’s about enjoying the atmosphere and finding peace while discovering something different.

What makes traveling even more fun for me is creating cinematic content along the way. I like bringing my camera or even just my phone to capture moments that tell a story. Things like slow-motion shots, golden sunsets, or little details that most people might not notice. Those small things can look so much more beautiful when filmed in a cinematic style. Later, when I edit the clips and add music, it feels like I’m turning the trip into a short movie. Seeing the final video gives me a sense of satisfaction, especially when it captures the vibe exactly how I felt it.

Another reason I love traveling and making cinematic videos is because it’s the best way to keep memories alive. Photos are nice, but videos feel more alive, you can see and feel the atmosphere again when you watch them later. Plus, I enjoy sharing them with friends or on social media so others can “travel” with me through the video. Sometimes people even get inspired to visit those places themselves. In the end, traveling helps me stay relaxed, and making cinematic videos makes the whole experience even more meaningful and exciting.
